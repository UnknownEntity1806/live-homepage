Making a game: dev diary #3

May 30, 2026

Changing plans

So, last time I said that I would first make all the necessary assets… Well, seems like that is not the right approach. As what I mostly need to learn is working in Godot, it’s better to use placeholder assets first. So that I understand the mechanics and write the code first. Then, if I mess something up it’s not such a bad feeling as to when you already have the final graphics in place. And I am really grateful to the r/Godot community, who keeps giving me new inisghts and stuff to learn. Also, everyone that shares assets for free on itch.io is so kind! It really helps me to be able to jump into learning to program the game instead of having to put a lot of time into assets at the start of this project. And about AI, I really moved away from using it to learn Godot, as it just is not working for me to learn in that way. I learn much better by just trying and then looking actively for solutions when I hit a wall.

The process so far

As of now I have the basic structure set up in Godot for the different screen in the game. And I have been first working on the StartScreen, as this was the one with the least features. So just a little bit of coding required. It has already thaught me a lot. Like, I know what I want to achieve, I just do not know how to get there yet. So I spend a lot of time switching between Godot, the Godot Documentation and just plain old Google search. I now know how to implement sprite animation sheets, how to insert text labels. How to connect buttons to the script. And it’s been fun! I kinda forgot how much I enjoy learning something that has my interest.

Combining stories

My overall vision is to combine the magical fairy world with a solarpunk modern world in my game-world. At first I was not sure how to do this, as @itsCloudyDay and the Walk In The Forest world seem so far apart. But I have found a way it will make sense in the worldbuilding 😁 I do not want to give away too much yet, as it will only really become relevant if this all takes off. But I will share that the idea is that Chloe and the fairy Moira have known each other since childhood 💜
